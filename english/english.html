English Grammar

Parts of Speech

Nouns: Teach that nouns are names of people, places, things, or animals.
Example: "Cat, house, Mary."

Verbs: Actions or states of being.
Example: "Run, jump, is, are."

Adjectives: Describe nouns.
Example: "Big, red, happy."

Adverbs: Describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.
Example: "Quickly, very, often."

Pronouns: Words that replace nouns.
Example: "He, she, it, they."

Sentence Structure

Subject: The person or thing doing the action.
Example: "The dog (subject) barks."

Predicate: The action or description.
Example: "The dog barks loudly (predicate)."

Articles: "A," "an," and "the" are used with nouns.
Example: "A cat, an apple, the sun."

Punctuation

Capital letters: Start sentences or proper nouns.

Full stop/Period (.): End a sentence.

Comma (,): Separate items in a list.

Question mark (?): End a question.

Exclamation mark (!): Show excitement or strong emotion.

Singular and Plural

Singular: One object/person.
Example: "Apple."

Plural: More than one.
Example: "Apples." Rules:

Add -s (cat → cats).
Add -es (bus → buses).
Irregular: Child → children.

Basic Questions

Who, what, where, when, why, how.

Example: "Where is the dog?"

Articles (A, An, The)

"A" for singular nouns starting with consonants.
"An" for singular nouns starting with vowels.
"The" for specific nouns.

Capitalization

Capitalize:
The first word in a sentence.

Names of people, places, days, months.

Example: "We went to Paris on Monday."

Word Order

Subject → Verb → Object

Example: "The dog (subject) chased (verb) the ball (object)."

Tenses

Past: "I played."

Present: "I play."
